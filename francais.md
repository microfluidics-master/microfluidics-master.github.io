The Microfluidics Master delivers **high‑level technological training** through:

- **Hands‑on lab classes** on the IPGG technological platform;
- A **Master’s project** in research or R&D;
- A written **Master’s thesis** and defense.

**Workload**: *60 ECTS*, from **early September** to **late June/July** (depending on internship/thesis schedule).

### Typical modules
- Fundamentals of microfluidics and soft matter
- Microfabrication and miniaturization
- Imaging, data & instrumentation
- Applications in life sciences and materials

More details are provided during the orientation at the start of the semester.
